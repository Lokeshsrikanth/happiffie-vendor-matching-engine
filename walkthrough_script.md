# Loom Walkthrough Recording Script

Use this script as a 2-3 minute blueprint for your screen recording submission.

---

## Preparation
1. Ensure the backend and frontend are running: `npm run dev`.
2. Open your browser to [http://localhost:3000/](http://localhost:3000/).
3. Clear/restart browser page state so you start on the **Client Matching Feed** tab.

---

## Act 1: Introduction (0:00 - 0:30)
- **Action**: Show your webcam/face or just hover cursor over the header logo.
- **Script**:
  > *"Hi everyone, this is a quick walkthrough of the Happiffie AI Vendor Matching Engine. Happiffie connects event organizers with service providers. Today I'm going to demonstrate how we use Semantic pgvector embeddings, Claude LLM agents, and rate-limiting constraints to solve matching at scale."*
- **Action**: Hover cursor over the **AI Plain-English Intake** box on the left.
- **Script**:
  > *"To show the system in action, let's start with a customer requirement. Instead of forcing users to fill out complex forms, we let them enter details in plain English."*

---

## Act 2: AI Intake & Semantic Matching (0:30 - 1:15)
- **Action**: Click the **AI Auto-Fill Form** button next to the textarea: *"Need a traditional south-indian wedding caterer in Bangalore for 300 guests on 12th Oct 2026, budget around 1.5 lakhs"*.
- **Action**: Watch the form pre-populate immediately. Review fields.
- **Script**:
  > *"Clicking 'AI Auto-fill' triggers a call to Anthropic's Claude. It automatically parsed out the event date, guest count of 300, a budget of 1.5 lakhs, and set the city to Bangalore and category to Caterer. Let's submit this."*
- **Action**: Click **Run Matching Query**. Scroll down the Match list.
- **Script**:
  > *"Upon submission, our matching engine runs. It first filters candidates deterministically on city, dates, and budget floors. Then, we use real semantic sentence embeddings. We fetch vendor portfolio vectors from a PostgreSQL pgvector database and compute cosine similarities, replacing fragile keyword search. Notice that the match cards show specific, real-time explanation sentences generated by Claude and cached in the database for optimal latency."*

---

## Act 3: Rate Limiting & Vendor Simulation (1:15 - 1:45)
- **Action**: Scroll to show the top candidate cards. Point out the invitation states.
- **Script**:
  > *"The top 3 matches automatically receive invites. To protect vendor quality, we enforce a strict rolling cap of 5 invites per 24 hours. If a popular vendor reaches their limit, they are skipped and labeled, allowing the request to slide seamlessly to the next eligible tier. Let's simulate one vendor accepting the lead."*
- **Action**: Under the first matching vendor, input a quote of ₹1,40,000 and click **Accept & Book**. Watch the green success banner popup.
- **Script**:
  > *"Accepting immediately books the vendor, records the contract value, locks their calendar for Oct 12th, and graduates them from cold-start discovery status."*

---

## Act 4: Admin Interventions & Audit Trails (1:45 - 2:20)
- **Action**: Switch to the **Admin Control** tab. Select the topmost requirement in the log.
- **Script**:
  > *"Now let's switch to the platform Admin Panel. Here, admins can audit candidate pools and apply manual overrides: boosting a local partner, forcing an invitation, or excluding poor matches."*
- **Action**: Find the second vendor in the list and click **Boost (+20 pts)**.
- **Script**:
  > *"Let's apply a score boost. In a live marketplace, overrides can cause disputes, so we log everything. Scroll down to see our real-time Manual Adjustments History table. This audit trail tracks timestamps, action types, delta score changes, and reasons for complete business auditability."*

---

## Act 5: Analytics Dashboard & Conclusion (2:20 - 2:45)
- **Action**: Switch to the **Metrics Dashboard** tab.
- **Script**:
  > *"Finally, we look at the Metrics Dashboard. We aggregate live platform KPIs including vendor response rates, average reply speeds, and final booking conversion rates. We also render a dynamic score distribution histogram for the last matching pool, showing the distribution of Excellent vs. Poor candidate matches."*
- **Action**: Scroll back to header.
- **Script**:
  > *"This combination of semantic relevance, deterministic rate caps, and transparent admin audit logs provides a robust, production-quality matching engine. Thank you!"*
